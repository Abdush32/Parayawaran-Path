Types of Power Stations and Their Role in Energy Generation

Power stations, also known as power plants or energy stations, are facilities used for the generation of electrical Power. They are usually located near fuel sources and are designed to convert different forms of energy into electrical energy. Power stations are the backbone of modern society, providing the electricity needed to power homes, businesses, and industries.

  • Thermal Power Stations
  • Nuclear Power Stations
  • Hydropower Stations

These are the most common type, where heat energy from burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, or natural gas is used to produce steam, which drives turbines connected to electricity generators.
